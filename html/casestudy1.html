Easy Chef - Simplifying Recipe Searching for Everyone

The people who are new to cooking need a method to find out the difficulty level of a recipe in order to know whether they can manipulate it without actually starting cooking it.

Solution

Easy Chef is a website that provides users with an extensive database of recipes, personalized cookbooks, and the ability to search for recipes with filters based on dietary restrictions, allergies, difficulty, and cost.

Background

Meals are important in people's daily life. But cooking can be frustrating for many people, especially those who are new to it and do not have too much time to spend on it. They may need to spend a lot of time to find the right recipes and learn to cook. It is also hard to find a recipe for both simple and tasty food with personal preference. As a result, many people may choose to eat fast food, expensive takeout or pre-packaged meals.

Easy Chef was a simple and user-friendly recipe website to address those problems by providing users with a wide variety of simple, delicious, and healthy recipes. By providing a personalized cookbook, users could search recipes with filters based on dietary restrictions, allergies, difficulty, and cost. It makes cooking enjoyable for those busy students or people who are new to cooking.

Behind the Scenes of Our Software Development - The Decision Making Journey That Shaped Our Project

Decision 1: Use Cloud or Local Database Storage

Context and Problem Statement: In order to provide a seamless experience for users accessing recipes, our team needed to decide on the most effective way to store them. The primary consideration was whether to use cloud or local database storage.

Considered Options: We considered both cloud and local database storage as potential options for storing the recipes. Local storage would have the advantage of being more secure, as the data would be stored on the user's own device. However, this would limit the user's ability to access the recipes from multiple devices, which was an important consideration. On the other hand, cloud storage would enable users to easily access their recipes from any device with an internet connection, making it more convenient for users who use multiple devices.

Decision Outcome: After careful consideration, we ultimately decided to use cloud storage for storing the recipes. The decision was based on several factors, including the need for users to access their recipes from any device, as well as the ability to easily save recipes for meal plans on the cloud. This choice would provide users with greater flexibility and convenience in accessing and managing their recipes.

Decision 2: Types of Filters

Context and Problem Statement: We aimed to provide a user-friendly experience for users when searching for recipes on the website. In order to achieve this, we needed to decide on the most relevant and useful types of filters to include.

Considered Options: We considered several options for types of filters to include on the website, including type of cuisine, time, cost, meal of the day, dietary restrictions, and main ingredient. Type of cuisine was considered as a potential filter, as it can help users narrow down their search based on their culinary preferences. Time was also considered, as it can help users quickly find recipes that can be prepared within their available time frame. Cost was an important factor to consider, as the target audience for the website are college students, who may be more budget-conscious. Meal of the day was considered to help users find recipes that fit a specific meal time. Dietary restrictions were also considered as a potential filter, as many users have specific dietary requirements. Finally, the main ingredient was also considered as a filter, as it can help users narrow down their search based on their preferred ingredients.

Decision Outcome: After careful consideration, we decided to include filters for time, cost, dietary restrictions, and main ingredient. Time was chosen as a filter as it is important information for users when deciding whether to make a recipe, especially for our target audience of college students who are likely to be time-constrained. Cost was chosen as a filter as users should know how much a recipe costs, especially for our target audience. Dietary restrictions were selected as a filter to show our users that their restrictions are being considered, as it is important for users with specific dietary needs to be able to find appropriate recipes. Finally, the main ingredient was chosen as it is the most common criteria users would filter by, making it an essential filter for providing an effective search experience.

Decision 3: Include or Not Include Sidebar Quick Search Function

Context and Problem Statement: In our project, we aimed to improve the efficiency of user searches on our recipe website. We considered whether or not to include a quick search function in the sidebar.

Considered Options: Our team discussed including a quick search function in the sidebar as well as not including it.

Decision Outcome: After careful consideration, we decided not to include a quick search function in the sidebar. We recognized that the search bar was already present and easily accessible from every page, so a quick search function would be redundant. Additionally, it might have required a lot of coding effort to only save a few clicks, which we deemed not necessary. By not including the sidebar quick search function, we were able to simplify our design and reduce potential coding complexity while still providing users with efficient search options through the search bar.
